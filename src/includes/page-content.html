10 Steps to Adoption

1 Learn About Adoption

Adoption is a lifelong commitment, so learning the ins and outs of the adoption process is privy to becoming adoptive parents. Do your research on infant adoption and decide what will work best for you.

2 Contact Everlasting Adoptions

Everlasting Adoptions is a great place to start your adoption journey. Our organization consists of the owner, who is an adoptive mother herself, and a knowledgeable team who is passionate about adoption. Included in our services is the creation of a custom, hard copy profile, a digital profile on our website, and connections to social workers, attorneys, financial officers, and, of course, your birthmother. We will guide you every step of the way, and we are available to you day, evening and even weekends by text, email or phone.

3 Complete an Adoption Home Study

The completion of an adoption home study is required in order to become licensed to adopt in the United States. But, you do not have to have an approved home study before you join Everlasting Adoptions. Once you are accepted into our program, we will direct you to professional social workers, in your area, who will conduct your home study.

4 Create an Adoption Profile

As part of our marketing efforts to match you with a birthmother, Everlasting Adoptions has a professional profile creator that will work with you to design custom adoption profile(s) - hard copies as well as a digital profile on our website. The adoption profile paints a picture of your home, family, relationship and hobbies, and allows you to express your excitement about growing your family through adoption. It is your main marketing tool. We make edits and updates to your profile as necessary, and replenish copies, free of charge.

5 Consider the Criteria for the Type of Adoption You Want.

We maintain a large database for our prospective adoptive parents. We will ask you to give us your requirements with regard to information such as all of the race combinations of the baby you are willing to raise, whether you want a boy or a girl, the type of communication you are comfortable with after birth, and the amount of financial support you might be able to budget for to help her during her pregnancy. We take all of these requirements, enter them in our database and then begin the match-making process. It is important to give some thought to the criteria you will be comfortable with.

6 Waiting for Your Birthmother

Because the owner is an adoptive parent herself, we know this is the most difficult part of the process. This is the time to be patient and exercise positivity and hope. Remember, you can create your reality!

7 Communicating with your Birthmother

You will be notified by text, email and phone call that we have a birthmother who is interested in learning more about you. You will receive a summary of information, and decide whether or not you want to further engage. If so, you will develop a relationship by texting, emailing, and phone calling, that hopefully leads to a face to face visit. This visit usually confirms that this a great match or perhaps reveals that a better match is waiting. If confirmed and positive, a legal professional will be hired in the birthmothers state by the adoptive parents.

After delivery, you must wait for the parental relinquishment hours to pass (usually 72 hour or less) and for the Interstate Compact Placement of Children (ICPC) to clear. Baby is typically in your possession after discharge, but sometimes a birthmother will take the baby with her during this waiting period.

10 Bring Your Baby Home

Once your baby is born, parental relinquishments have been signed, and the ICPC has cleared, you can take baby back home to your state. A decision is usually discussed between you and the legal professionals that were hired.
